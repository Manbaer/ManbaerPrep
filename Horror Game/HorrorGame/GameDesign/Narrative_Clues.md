# Narrative Clues

This is the story bible for every piece of in-game text. It records what each clue says, where it appears, and what it is allowed to imply.

Written as part of CurrentTask 14. See [[Core_Premise]], [[Real_World_Consequences]], and [[Ending_Concepts]].

## The Two Readings

Every clue must support both of these readings without confirming either:

1. **The caretaker reading.** The player lived here with someone smaller than them - a sibling or a child. That person was sick. The player was their caretaker. Six years ago the person went to the hospital, or should have, and did not come back. The player was going to leave afterward (the bus ticket) and never did. The dreams are memory forcing its way back in.

2. **The patient reading.** The player is the one who does not wake. Chart 204 is theirs; the handwriting on the medical transfer is theirs; the house is the shape their sleep has taken. The "someone else" in the clues is whoever is waiting for them outside the sleep. The outside world glimpsed through the front door is just another aisle of the dream.

Rules to keep both readings alive:

- Never name anyone. "J. DOE" only.
- Never state a death. Nothing says died, funeral, or grave.
- The only number allowed is "six years."
- The other person is only ever evidence-sized: a small voice, a child-sized bracelet, a second bed, a worn-away face.
- The player's own handwriting keeps appearing where someone else's should be.

## The Five Questions

- **Who is the player?** Someone who stopped leaving the house six years ago. Caretaker or patient - never resolved.
- **What is the house?** A place that rewrites itself to match what the player is ready to remember. It behaves like a mind.
- **What happened at the hospital?** Someone connected to this house was a patient. Care was transferred home, and home closed over it.
- **Who used to live here?** Someone small. Goodnights were said. They filmed things. Their room is no longer on the floor plan.
- **Why did the player stop leaving?** The bus ticket says they nearly did, six years ago. Whatever they would have had to leave behind - or wake up from - kept them here.

## Emotional Setup For The Final Act

By the end of day 5 the player has been told, in fragments: there were two of you, one of you was sick, one of you does not wake, and you were going to leave once. The final act (CurrentTask 15) can now let the house merge with the dreams, and the three endings become three answers: leave (use the ticket at last), stay (keep the house closed), sleep (go where the other person is).

## Consistency Checklist

- Six years appears in: clinic message, radio countdown (six), bus ticket, drawer ticket. Never any other number. ✓
- The errand list (milk, batteries, clinic) appears in: kitchen note, gas station receipt, battery item text. ✓
- Goodnight appears in: after-dream machine message, wheat field fuse clue ("goodnight" = Moon). ✓
- The child appears only as: small voice, child-sized bracelet, worn-face photo, waving figure, small drawn bed. Never described directly. ✓
- No clue names a person, a date, or a cause. ✓
