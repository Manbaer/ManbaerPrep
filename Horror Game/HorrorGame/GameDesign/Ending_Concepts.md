# Ending Concepts

The ending should not feel like the player defeated a monster. It should feel like the player finally understands what the house, dreams, and routine were doing.

Theme:

Isolation -> routine -> dreams -> reality changing -> truth leaking through

The final question is:

> Can you open the front door?

See [[Core_Premise]], [[Dream_Environments]], and [[Real_World_Consequences]] for the story setup.

## Best Ending Concept: The House Was Preserving a Loop

Near the end, the player discovers the house is not just haunted. The house is acting like a memory machine.

Every dream environment represents a piece of the player's life:

- Endless hallway = avoidance and trying to return to safety
- Wheat field = power, distance, and the outside world
- Hospital = illness, care, and buried truth
- Gas station = leaving, travel, and escape

The house keeps forcing the player through dreams because the player has forgotten, hidden, or refused to face something important.

The horror is not only that the house is evil. The horror is that the house is trying to keep the player exactly where they are.

Or stronger:

The player helped create the loop because routine was easier than moving on.

## Final Act Structure

### 1. The House Stops Pretending

After the final dream, the player wakes up, but the house is obviously wrong:

- The bedroom door opens into the gas station aisle.
- The hallway windows show the wheat field.
- The bathroom has hospital lighting.
- The TV plays footage of the player sleeping.
- The answering machine plays messages from different dates.
- The front door opens into the endless hallway.

This tells the player that the dreams are no longer separate from the real world.

### 2. Revisit Each Dream Object

The ending should use objects from each dream as keys:

- Bedroom hallway: clock
- Wheat field: fuse
- Hospital: patient file
- Gas station: receipt

Possible final puzzle:

1. Put the receipt on the kitchen table.
2. Put the fuse in the basement box.
3. Put the patient file beside the bed.
4. Set the clock to the correct time.
5. Play the VHS tape.

Once everything is placed, the front door unlocks.

## Main Ending Choice

The final choice should be made naturally through movement:

- Walk outside = Leave the House
- Close the door = Stay Inside
- Go back upstairs to bed = Go Back to Sleep

No big menu choice is needed.

## Ending 1: Leave the House, Morning

This is the closest thing to the good ending.

The player opens the front door and walks outside. For the first time, there is no impossible hallway, no wheat field, and no gas station aisle. Just a normal street in the early morning.

The player turns around and sees the house behind them. One upstairs bedroom light is still on.

Meaning:

The player has not fixed everything. They simply broke the loop. Recovery and change are not dramatic here. They are the first step outside.

Final image:

- The player walks down the street.
- The sun rises.
- The house remains behind them.
- The answering machine rings once inside the empty house.
- Cut to black.

This ending should feel quiet, not triumphant.

## Ending 2: Stay Inside, Routine

The player refuses to leave. They close the front door. The house returns to normal.

The next morning begins exactly like the first day:

- Same bed
- Same TV
- Same chores

But small things are worse:

- The hallway is slightly longer.
- The bedroom door has no handle.
- The calendar has no dates.
- The answering machine says, "You chose this."

Then the player goes to bed again. The game ends with the first dream hallway starting over.

Meaning:

The player chooses comfort over truth. This is the bad ending, but it should feel sad and numb, not cartoonishly evil.

## Ending 3: Go Back to Sleep, Dream House

Instead of leaving, the player goes back to bed. They wake up in a perfect version of the house:

- Warm lighting
- Clean rooms
- No decay
- No strange sounds

But every window shows a different dream:

- One window shows the wheat field.
- One window shows the hospital hallway.
- One window shows the gas station.
- One window shows the endless bedroom corridor.

The player has not escaped. They have chosen the dream permanently.

Meaning:

This is the comforting nightmare ending. The dream gives the player peace, but only by removing reality.

Final line from the TV or answering machine:

> Stay as long as you need.

Then the house lights turn off one by one.

## Secret Ending: Open the Front Door Early

The player tries to leave the house before solving everything.

At first, the front door is locked or leads to blackness. After hidden conditions are met, the player can open it early.

Outside is not a street. It is the endless wheat field with electrical poles.

Meaning:

The outside world has already become part of the dream. This ending is not good or bad. It is a disturbing lore ending.

## Recommended Canon Ending

Use Leave the House as the canon ending, but keep it ambiguous.

The player walks outside. The sun rises. They are finally away from the house.

Then the camera slowly turns back to the bedroom window.

Inside, the player's bed is still visible.

Someone is sleeping in it.

Cut to black.

This leaves the player asking:

- Did I escape?
- Was the sleeping person the old version of me?
- Was I the dream version?
- Did the house let me go, or copy me?

The game should not end with a clean explanation. It should end with a strong emotional image.

## Strong Ending Symbol

Use the front door as the final symbol:

- Bedroom door = comfort
- Dream doors = memory
- Hospital doors = truth
- Gas station door = escape
- Front door = real change

The whole game builds toward one question:

> Can you open the front door?
