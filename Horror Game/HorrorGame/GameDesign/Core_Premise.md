# Core Premise

The player is a shut-in living alone in a 1990s house. At first, it seems like they have a strange sleep disorder or repeating nightmare. Over time, the player discovers that their dreams are connected to the house, the bedroom, the phone, a hospital, a past event, and the reason they stopped leaving.

The central mystery is:

> Why does the house change every time I dream?

The deeper question is:

> Am I escaping the house, or is the house using my dreams to rebuild itself?

## Main Idea

During the day, the player completes simple tasks inside the house:

- Wake up
- Eat
- Watch TV
- Check the answering machine
- Do chores
- Go to bed

At night, the player enters dream spaces:

- [[Dream_Environments#Endless Bedroom Hallway]]
- [[Dream_Environments#Infinite Wheat Field With Electrical Poles]]
- [[Dream_Environments#Hospital Dream]]
- [[Dream_Environments#Infinite Gas Station]]

Actions in the dreams affect the real house. A dream is not just a cool level. Each dream should represent something in the real world, then change the house when the player wakes up.

## Core Mechanic

The game should be built around this chain:

Dream environment
-> dream puzzle
-> symbolic meaning
-> real-world consequence
-> new house change or story flag

The house should read the player's completed dreams through [[Story_Flags]] and update itself through [[Real_World_Consequences]].

## Story Interpretation

The dreams are connected to:

- The house
- The bedroom
- The phone
- The hospital
- A past event
- Someone who used to live there
- The reason the player stopped leaving

The game should keep this ambiguous. Possible truths include:

- The player may have been caring for someone.
- The house may have belonged to a sick family member.
- The player may be avoiding a real medical or emotional truth.
- The house may be preserving a loop because change is too painful.
- The player may have helped create the loop because routine was easier than moving on.

## Theme

Isolation -> routine -> dreams -> reality changing -> truth leaking through

The ending should not feel like the player defeated a monster. It should feel like the player finally understands what the house, dreams, and routine were doing.

See [[Ending_Concepts]] for possible endings.
