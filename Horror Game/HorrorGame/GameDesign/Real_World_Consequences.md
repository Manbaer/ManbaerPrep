# Real World Consequences

The house should change after each dream. The player should feel that the dream did something permanent, even if the change is small.

Core rule:

> Dreams set flags. The house reads flags. The house changes itself.

See [[Dream_Environments]] for the dream designs and [[Story_Flags]] for proposed flag names.

## Consequence Structure

Each dream consequence should answer:

- What did the player fix, break, find, or choose in the dream?
- What object, room, sound, message, or route changes in the house?
- What new story flag is now true?
- What new interaction is now possible?

## Bedroom Hallway Consequences

Dream actions:

- Fix the painting.
- Answer the phone.
- Stop the clock.
- Cover the mirror.
- Reach the bedroom door.

Real-world changes:

- A photo in the bedroom changes.
- The answering machine has a new message.
- All clocks show the same impossible time.
- The bathroom mirror is covered with a sheet.
- The bedroom door is slightly open.
- The bedsheets are messy.
- A new photo appears on the dresser.
- The closet door unlocks.

Purpose:

The bedroom should feel less safe after the first major dream.

## Wheat Field Consequences

Dream actions:

- Restore the circuit.
- Follow the humming.
- Activate the correct electrical poles.
- Power the distant house.

Real-world changes:

- Basement light now works.
- A previously dark room can be entered.
- TV turns on by itself.
- TV shows static even when off.
- Kitchen light flickers.
- Electrical buzzing can be heard inside the walls.
- A radio starts receiving strange audio.

Possible unlocks:

- Basement
- Garage
- Utility room
- Locked hallway
- Fuse room

Purpose:

The dream should unlock a new real-world area and make the house's electrical systems feel alive.

## Hospital Consequences

Dream actions:

- Find the patient file.
- Unlock the correct hospital room.
- Retrieve a medical item.
- Activate a machine or memory scene.
- Discharge the missing patient.

Real-world changes:

- A medical document appears on the kitchen table.
- A locked room gains hospital wallpaper or fluorescent lighting.
- The house phone receives a call from a hospital.
- The bedroom gains a hospital privacy curtain.
- The bathroom cabinet contains old medication bottles.
- The kitchen table has a medical bill.
- A locked drawer opens.

Purpose:

The dream should reveal medical, caretaking, or identity-related backstory without confirming one clean explanation.

## Gas Station Consequences

Dream actions:

- Buy batteries.
- Buy a VHS tape.
- Buy a map.
- Buy a key.
- Print the receipt with the player's home address.

Real-world changes:

- A receipt appears on the kitchen counter.
- The fridge contains gas station food.
- The TV remote works.
- A VHS tape appears beside the TV and can be played.
- A map appears on the wall and shows impossible rooms.
- A locked drawer can open.
- The front door briefly opens to an impossible road.

Purpose:

The gas station should unlock items, not mainly rooms. It should make the player feel closer to leaving while also making the outside world less trustworthy.

## Final Act Consequences

After the final dream, the house stops pretending that dreams and reality are separate:

- The bedroom door opens into the gas station aisle.
- Hallway windows show the wheat field.
- The bathroom has hospital lighting.
- The TV plays footage of the player sleeping.
- The answering machine plays messages from different dates.
- The front door opens into the endless hallway.

The final playable section can be called:

> The House After Sleep

It combines every environment:

Bedroom -> hallway loop -> kitchen -> hospital ward -> basement fuse room -> wheat field window -> gas station counter -> front door

The player collects or places one object from each dream, then the front door unlocks.
